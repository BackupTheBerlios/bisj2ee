Research of the group includes:
  • The study and development of languages, models and systems that guide the coordination of activities in networked systems. These coordinations languages follow an uncoupled style of interaction in the fashion of the coordination language Linda and are targeted at networked and Internet-based environments.
  • The study, application and evaluation of XML-technologies for networkbased information systems, amongst others the study and application of Semantic Web technologies.
  • The use of novel models for the coordination of agent societies at a huge scale, especially those models based in Swarm Intelligence.
Funded projects on Semantic Web
  • Semantic Web in der Pathologie, in cooperaton with Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin and Universität Potsdam. An information retrieval system for images and text based on Semantic Web technologies for pathology focusing on lungdiseases.
  • Wissensnetze, Project A of the Berlin Research Centre for the Internet Economy InterVal. Studies  the impact of semantic technologies from the business and the technical viewpoint to make predictions about the influence of the new technologies on markets, enterprises and individuals.
  • KnowledgeWeb, Network of Excellence, EU FP6. Realizing the Semantic Web
  • Member of OntoWeb, a European Union founded project about Ontology-based information exchange for knowledge management and electronic commerce.
  • Member of Agentlink III, Network of Excellence in Agent research, EU.
Funded projects on XML Technologies
  • XML Sicherheitsdienste für GBIF-D, funded by BMBF, in cooperation with The Botanischer Garten und Botanisches Museum Berlin. Providing XML security services and access controls for the German node of the Global Biodiversity Information Facility.
  • SYNTHESYS, funded by the EU, in cooperation with The Botanischer Garten und Botanisches Museum Berlin. Develop authentication services for system access in DiGIR/ABCD, so that restricted data access can be granted to appropriate end-users.
  • XML Clearinghouse Berlin-Brandenburg, funded by BMBF in cooperation with Humboldt Universität zu Berlin. Knowledge transfer on XML technology focused on the region Berlin-Brandenburg.
  • XML Lab, funded by Microsoft Research Cambridge (currently being extended from TU Berlin to FU Berlin). An infrastructure for teaching XML technologies.
  • XMLSpaces.net, funded by Microsoft Research Cambridge. An implementation of XMLSpaces on the .net platform.
Funded projects on Swarming
  • SwarmLinda, funded in part by DAAD/NSF. A distributed coordination system based on principles of swarm intelligence
Current research results are 
  • Workspaces, a Web-based workflow system
  • XMLSpaces, a distributed store for XML-documents
  • The Dependency Markup Language DML
  • The object-oriented distributed language dSelf which is based on the prototype and delegation principles.
